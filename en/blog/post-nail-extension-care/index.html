Post-Nail Extension Care

June 17, 2026 Calculating... Care

Nail extension is an investment in your beauty. But to make this beauty last and protect your natural nails, you must follow post-extension care carefully. In this article, we'll teach you 10 golden tips that will significantly increase the longevity of your nail extensions.

10 Golden Tips for Nail Extension Care

Below are the most important tips to follow after getting nail extensions, listed in order of importance. By following these tips, your nail extensions will last up to 4 weeks.

1. Don't Wet Your Nails for the First 24 Hours

Most important tip! For the first 24 hours after extension, don't submerge your nails in water. The extension materials need this time to fully harden and bond with your natural nails. Water can weaken the adhesion and reduce longevity.

  • Avoid swimming pools, hot tubs, and long baths
  • When washing hands, be quick and keep nails dry
  • If you must wash dishes, wear gloves

2. Don't Use Your Nails as Tools

Extensions are durable, but they are not tools! Never use your nails to open cans, peel stickers, or as screwdrivers. These actions can cause breakage, separation, or damage to natural nails.

Warning

Using your nail extensions as tools is one of the most common reasons for breakage and separation. Please avoid this habit.

3. Wear Gloves When Using Cleaning Products

Cleaning agents and chemical removers can gradually damage your nail extensions and cause dullness. Always wear gloves when working with these products.

  • Rubber or nitrile gloves are the best option
  • After using cleaning products, wash hands with lukewarm water
  • Avoid direct contact with alcohol and acetone

4. Apply Cuticle Oil Daily

Cuticle oil is the best friend of your nail extensions! It keeps the skin around your nails soft and prevents dryness and cracking. It also helps increase the longevity of your extensions.

  • Apply cuticle oil at least twice daily (morning and night)
  • Gently massage the oil around the cuticle area
  • You can use natural oils like almond or jojoba oil

5. Visit the Salon for Maintenance Every 2-3 Weeks

Due to natural nail growth, after 2-3 weeks a gap forms between the extension and the cuticle. To prevent moisture and bacteria from entering this gap, you should get fill-ins (maintenance) done. This also increases the longevity of your extensions.

6. File Nails Gently

If your extensions get chipped or uneven, never pinch or pull them forcefully. Use a soft file to reshape them. It's best to leave this to your technician.

7. Use Acetone-Free Nail Polish Remover

If you've applied polish on your extensions, use acetone-free polish remover to remove it. Acetone can damage the extension materials and weaken them.

8. Maintain a Healthy Diet

The health of your natural nails starts from within. For strong and healthy nails, maintain a diet rich in vitamins and minerals:

  • Protein: Meat, eggs, legumes
  • Vitamin E: Nuts, seeds, vegetable oils
  • Biotin: Eggs, bananas, avocados
  • Zinc: Red meat, pumpkin seeds, lentils

9. Visit the Salon if You Feel Pain or Sensitivity

If you experience unusual pain, pressure, or sensitivity after extension, it could be a sign of a problem. The extension might be too tight or an infection could be forming. In these cases, visit the salon immediately.

Warning Signs
  • Redness and swelling around the nail
  • Persistent or throbbing pain
  • Separation of the extension from the natural nail
  • Discoloration (green or dark spots)

10. Have Extensions Removed by a Professional

Never pull off your nail extensions! This will seriously damage your natural nails. Always visit a salon for professional removal using specialized materials and techniques.

Care Schedule

After 3-4 weeks Professional removal at salon

Additional Tips

Seasonal Care

In summer, due to increased sweating, extensions may loosen faster. Be more diligent with hygiene care. In winter, the skin around your nails becomes dry, so apply cuticle oil more frequently.

Can I Exercise?

Yes, but in activities like weightlifting, tennis, or volleyball that put pressure on your hands, be sure to use protective gloves to prevent nail damage.

Can I Swim with Extensions?

After the first 24 hours, you can swim. However, dry your nails well after swimming and apply cuticle oil to remove excess moisture.

Quick Care Summary

  • Don't wet for 24 hours
  • Don't use as tools
  • Wear gloves when washing
  • Don't forget cuticle oil
  • Timely maintenance
  • Gentle filing
  • Acetone-free remover
  • Healthy nutrition
  • Monitor warning signs
  • Professional removal

"Caring for your nail extensions is a daily responsibility that, when followed, allows you to enjoy beautiful nails for up to 4 weeks. Remember, prevention is always better than cure."

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get a manicure after nail extension?

Yes, but it's better to leave this to your technician so it's done with proper tools and without damaging the extension.

Why do my nail extensions lift early?

There can be various reasons: excessive water exposure, using cleaning products without gloves, not getting timely maintenance, or using low-quality materials. Follow the tips in this article to increase longevity.

Do nail extensions thin natural nails?

If applied properly and removed in time, extensions won't damage natural nails. Problems arise when extensions are forcibly or improperly removed.

How often should I get maintenance?

Usually every 2-3 weeks, but this depends on your nail growth rate. Your technician will give you the best guidance.
